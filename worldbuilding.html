WORLDBUILDING
✦ HISTORY
In my universe, there are countries located in different regions that developed around the nire energy concentrated there (see power system). Thus, each nation has its own unique systems based on that energy. Historically, there were alliances and trade among all nations, with some choosing to remain isolated either from the rest of the world or even from parts of their own population. However, the monarch of the "ocean" country decided to implement a segregationist regime against any elemental species that was not human (including fantastical humanoids and beasts), as well as humans who could manipulate a nire element different from their country's own. At first, they began by capturing these groups and enforcing laws against them and against any nation that protected them. Later, they organized search parties into other regions, until the conflict escalated into war, with Nerith (the ocean country) invading other territories and attempting to conquer the remaining nations.
✦ COUNTRIES
Nerith
✦ CULTURE
Nerith has a strict male-focused culture, where women are viewed primarily as supporters of the male soldiers. Their clothing is inspired by old English attire, mostly centered around tones that reflect the colors of the ocean. They possess a strong navy and extensive trade networks with the few countries that still maintain relations with them. However, because of this, many rebels have chosen to challenge their naval power by forming forces of equal strength (yes, pirates).
✦ CURRENT ERA
Nowadays, Nerith is considered a threat to everyone due to its cruelty and its denial of humanity toward certain fantastical creatures. They have implemented a series of secret prisons meant to contain and eliminate these creatures. Over time, this led to the development of their current laboratories, dedicated to experimenting on both "beasts" and humans capable of manipulating two or more nire energies. Their goal is to strengthen the crown and ultimately develop a "cure".
Dummar
✦ CULTURE
Dummar is the country connected to earth nire. It has jungles on its western side and waterfalls and more tropical regions on its eastern side. In the north, along the border with Kharum, there is a vast desert region. The country is inspired by both Latin American and African customs. Dummar has a clear social divide between its regions, as the capital receives greater economic investment and stronger foreign influence, including clothing and cultural trends. In contrast, its other regions show resistance toward the system and choose to isolate themselves from the capital, functioning more like independent provinces.
✦ CURRENT ERA
Dummar chose to lift the barrier that separated them from the rest of the world. However, they maintain strict immigration policies and generally do not allow strangers inside. Their strategy is to ally themselves with vulnerable regions in order to spy on them and eventually take them down, as they are secretly allied with Nerith. Furthermore, they developed an agency in which their youth are trained in stages to create strong and loyal warriors. They operate by separating them into groups and assigning them identities of their own in the form of candidate numbers, allowing them to train together. At the same time, each child receives specialized training in either their chosen nire element or weapon. Moreover, there is a separate division dedicated to spying and eliminating threats, where agents work in pairs and follow extreme measures, such as not revealing their faces or identities even to their partner.
Iskral
✦ CULTURE
Iskral is the coldest country, situated in the far south. It is mostly covered in ice, and its coldest region is almost uninhabitable — this is where the fishing community is located. The country is inspired by Arctic communities and the colder regions of Mongolia. They have a strong connection to religion and believe in the Goddess of the Moon. They are guided by a priestess who is responsible for many decisions based on what the moon reveals to her. Its people wear her colors whenever the necessary dyes are available and adopt a more peaceful stance toward both the world and their own people.
✦ CURRENT ERA
The capital is located within an underground cave, where the castle serves as the center and foundation for all its people. There, they maintain farms and raise small animals, although they never go outside. On the surface, the people of Iskral are mostly nomads responsible for hunting in the more habitable regions, as well as inhabitants of small, hidden sedentary villages that provide fish to the capital. Finally, the hottest region of the country is responsible for farming and, in some cases, livestock production. People living on the surface are not allowed into the capital due to overcrowding. However, most do not resent this decision, as they only meet when supplies must be transported to the cave.
Stratos
✦ CULTURE
Stratos is the land with the highest concentration of air nire. Its people live in Ancient Greek-inspired cities built upon sky islands. However, there is a division among its population that has led to civil wars. There are two main islands: one inhabited by humans capable of manipulating air nire energy, and the other inhabited by several species of nire beasts, including different kinds of avian humanoids.
✦ CURRENT ERA
The humans believe them to be beasts and seek to purge them. That is why the avians were exiled to the other, deteriorated island, where they are constantly attacked by humans. The humans possess a strong military and control the main island, while the avians maintain a more peaceful stance and try to avoid conflict with them. That remained the case until the humans allied themselves with Nerith and captured and killed most of the avian population, leaving only a few survivors to escape to the other islands.
Kharum
✦ CULTURE
Kharum is the country with the highest concentration of fire nire. It is inspired by Middle Eastern and North African regions. They possess a strong military and a monarchy that has remained in power for centuries. They are known for their vast territory and the exploitation of their rich natural resources. They oppose Nerith; however, they do not plan on attacking directly. Instead, they host the largest rebel military movement against them. Contrary to many other countries, they believe their nire beasts are sacred. Kharum is also one of the most matriarchal countries and has a deep connection to its religion and culture, which are centered around the worship of the sun and fire nire. They maintain a strong connection to their ancestors and believe in the power of fire nire to protect both their people and their land.
✦ CURRENT ERA
Kharum has areas of land that were devastated by the war, leaving a large part of its population in poverty. As a result, various groups of scavengers formed in order to survive. They maintain a strong alliance with the newly established Leiha in order to protect their people against Nerith.
Leiha
✦ CULTURE
Led by nire creatures themselves, Leiha is a country formed by the survivors of the war against Nerith. The country was previously devastated both by the war and by attacks from various countries. It remained in ruins for decades until its leader reconstructed the land and provided a safe haven for victims of war. This is why, although it is known as the Land of Lightning, it contains a mixture of many cultures within itself. The country was inspired by both Chinese and Japanese cultures.
✦ CURRENT ERA
Now, Leiha has been reestablished as an opposition to Nerith and has been scheming with Kharum to take down the ocean country. They also hold strong beliefs in the protection of all nire creatures. As a form of inauguration, Leiha opened its gates to other countries through a festival held in honor of all elemental guardians (see powers page), as well as the cultures from which they originated. However, this celebration also serves as an excuse to host a meeting with Kharum and provides an opportunity for the other countries to send spies inside.
